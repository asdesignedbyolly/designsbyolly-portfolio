Aura Skincare — Email 01: Welcome & Convert
The brief

The first email in a 3-part welcome flow for a clean beauty DTC brand. Goal: convert the new subscriber with a welcome discount while establishing brand trust from the very first interaction.

Design decisions
  • Led with editorial hero image and large italic serif headline to signal premium positioning immediately
  • Promo code presented as a gift in a bordered box — ceremonial rather than transactional
  • Shipping and returns handled early to remove purchase friction before it arises
  • Carbon neutral delivery in the footer reinforces sustainability values subtly
The execution

Mobile-first at 600px. Every section background explicitly declared for dark mode. Two-column desktop layout with offer embedded in the hero right column.

Beverage DTC · Cold Audit · Before
OLIPOP — Dark Mode Rendering Gaps (Before)
The brief

After subscribing to OLIPOP’s email list, a systematic dark mode rendering failure was identified across their entire email programme — a structural ESP build problem affecting every send.

Design decisions
  • Black gaps between every section on dark mode devices — affecting ~80% of mobile opens
  • Root cause: no explicit background colours declared on section containers or spacer rows
  • Issue spans multiple email types — welcome, promotional, product emails all affected
  • Brands with strong visual identity suffer the most visible damage from this failure
The execution

This is the “before” that makes the “after” the pitch. Problem identified, documented, and presented as a cold audit with a ready-built solution.

OLIPOP — Welcome Email Redesign (After)
The brief

Full redesign of OLIPOP’s welcome email — solving the dark mode rendering problem while elevating the design to match the brand’s premium visual identity.

Design decisions
  • Every section background explicitly declared — zero transparent rows, zero gaps in any mode
  • Flavour card system redesigned for consistency — same proportions, treatment, and buttons across all cards
  • “You’re Fam Now” headline preserved — brand voice intact, layout elevated
  • Ingredients section on dark green creates contrast moment that signals real brand substance
The execution

Tested on dark canvas in Figma. Zero rendering gaps. Solves a technical problem and delivers a stronger brand experience simultaneously.

Process

How I
work

Step one
Discovery

Understanding your brand, audience, and goal before a single frame is opened. Async via brief form.

Step two
Audit

Reviewing existing emails and brand guidelines to find what works, what doesn’t, and where to go.

Step three
Design

Building in Figma — mobile first, dark mode ready, one clear action per email.

Step four
Refine

Two rounds of async revisions as standard. No calls required unless you want them.

Step five
Deliver

Figma source, exported assets, ESP-ready guidance. On time, every time.

FAQ

Common
questions

What exactly do you design?+
Email campaigns of all types — welcome flows, product launches, flash sales, cart abandonment, newsletters, and re-engagement sequences. If it lands in an inbox, I can design it.
Do you write the copy too?+
My focus is design, but I can work with your copy or refine basic copy as part of the brief.
What do you need to get started?+
Brand guidelines, existing email examples, copy, your ESP platform, and your deadline. I send a simple async brief form on day one.
Which platforms do you design for?+
All major ESPs — Klaviyo, Mailchimp, HubSpot, Omnisend, and others. I deliver Figma files plus export-ready assets.
How long does a project take?+
Single email: 3–5 business days. Welcome flow of 3 emails: 7–10 days. Rush turnarounds available.
How many revisions do I get?+
Two full async rounds included as standard. Additional rounds available at a small fee.
What’s your pricing?+
Single email from $250. Welcome flows from $600. Monthly retainers from $1,200. Dark mode audits from $150.
Where are you based?+
Ibadan, Nigeria (UTC/GMT+1). Remote-first and async-optimised — I work with brands globally with zero timezone friction.
